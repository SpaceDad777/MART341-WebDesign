Gluten Free Licorice Recipe

Licorice is one of my few favorite sweets. Since I cannot have wheat or gluten, that means I can't have it. I looked everywhere for gluten free licorice, unfortunately they all have some varient of wheat. So I decided to make my own batch that does not require wheat or any unecessary ingredients.


Ingredients

  • 1/2 cup of gluten free flour with xanthan gum
  • Pinch of salt
  • 1 teaspoon of Lorann cherry flavoring
  • Licorice root
  • 1 Stick of butter
  • 8 tablespoons of light corn syrup
  • 4 tablespoons of liquid sugar
  • 1 cup of sugar
  • 1/2 cup sweetened condensed milk
  • Hopes and dreams (optional)

What you will need

  • Medium mixing bowl
  • Sauce pan
  • Pizza cutter
  • Parchment paper
  • Mixing spoon
  • Candy thermometer
  • 9x9 Cake pan

Directions

  1. In a medium mixing bowl combine salt and flour. Mix well.
  2. In a sauce pan combine sweetened condensed milk, liquid sugar, corn syrup, butter, powdered licorice root and sugar.
  3. Over medium heat, bring pan's content to a boil, and cook until the temperature reaches 240 degrees.
  4. Use the candy thermomenter to make sure it does not pass the threshold.
  5. Remove pan from heat, and pour contents over flour mix.
  6. Mix well until smooth.
  7. Add cherry flavoring.
  8. pour contents into a greased pan.
  9. Place in fridge for 45 minutes to an hour or until chilled.
  10. Remove the licorice from the pan and place it on the parchment paper.
  11. Cut long strips of licorice or cut the long pieces into smaller pieces.
  12. Optional: For long strips of licorice, twist each piece.
  13. Grab a new piece of parchment paper and place the piece on it, or in a contianer so they can prevent from becoming stale.
